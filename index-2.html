PII Data Privacy Notice, Declaration & Informed Consent
Section 07 — Governing Law: DPDP Act 2023 & IT Act 2000 — Please read in full before submitting

1. Identity of the Data Fiduciary

Complynz ("the Company", "We", "Us") acts as the Data Fiduciary as defined under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 ("DPDP Act") in respect of all personal data collected through this form. By submitting this form, you ("the Data Principal") acknowledge that you have read, understood, and freely consented to the terms set out herein.

2. Classification of Personally Identifiable Information (PII) Collected

This form collects two categories of PII as defined under applicable data protection law:

Data Element PII Category Purpose of Collection
Full Name, Email, Phone Number Standard PII Identity & communication
Date of Birth Standard PII Age verification & records
Aadhaar Card Number / Document Sensitive PII Statutory identity verification
PAN Card Number / Document Sensitive PII Tax & financial compliance
Passport Size Photograph Sensitive PII Visual identity & ID badge
Academic Certificates / Marksheets Standard PII Credential verification
Emergency Contact Details Standard PII Emergency communication only
LinkedIn URL, Skills & Experience Standard PII Role suitability assessment

Note on Sensitive PII: Aadhaar-linked data is additionally governed by the Aadhaar (Targeted Delivery of Financial and Other Subsidies, Benefits and Services) Act, 2016 and UIDAI regulations. The Company collects a copy of the Aadhaar document solely for identity verification and does not store the 12-digit Aadhaar number in any searchable database.

3. Lawful Basis for Processing

The Company processes your personal data on the following lawful bases under the DPDP Act, 2023:

  • Consent (Section 6, DPDP Act): You provide free, informed, specific, and unambiguous consent by checking the boxes in this section.
  • Legitimate Use: Processing is necessary for the performance of the internship engagement and fulfilment of HR obligations.
  • Legal Obligation: Certain data (e.g., PAN) is required for compliance with the Income Tax Act, 1961 and other applicable statutes.

4. Purpose Limitation

Your personal data shall be used exclusively for the following purposes and for no other purpose without your fresh, explicit consent:

  • Verification of identity and eligibility for the internship
  • Preparation and execution of the internship letter / agreement
  • Statutory compliance including TDS, EPF/ESIC (if applicable)
  • Internal HR records, access credential issuance, and payroll processing
  • Issuance of internship completion certificate and official letters
  • Emergency contact use restricted solely to genuine emergency situations

5. Data Retention Period — 1 (One) Year

The Company shall retain your personal data, including all uploaded documents, for a period of one (1) year from the date of submission of this form or the date of conclusion of your internship, whichever is later ("Retention Period"). This retention period is determined based on:

  • Statutory requirement to maintain employment and engagement records
  • Resolution of any potential disputes arising from the internship engagement
  • Issuance of experience or reference letters post-internship

6. Data Security & Access Controls

The Company implements the following technical and organisational measures to protect your PII:

  • Role-based access control — only authorised HR personnel may access your data
  • All uploaded documents are stored in encrypted cloud storage
  • No personal data is stored on unencrypted local devices or personal email accounts
  • Access logs are maintained for all data retrieval activities
  • Third-party processors (e.g., cloud storage providers) are bound by data processing agreements

7. Non-Disclosure & Third-Party Sharing

The Company shall not sell, rent, license, trade, or disclose your personal data to any third party for commercial gain. Your data may be shared strictly on a need-to-know basis with:

  • Authorised HR and management personnel of Complynz
  • Government authorities or law enforcement agencies, if required by law or court order
  • Auditors or legal advisors, under strict confidentiality obligations

In all cases of third-party sharing mandated by law, you will be informed wherever legally permissible.
